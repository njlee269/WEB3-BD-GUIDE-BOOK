# BD 2026 Guidebook

## Who this is for

This book is for a business developer working in web3, blockchain, or digital assets who wants to operate with sharper commercial judgment in 2026.

It is written for someone who needs both:

- a global view of where real demand, policy support, and buyer budgets are moving
- a Korea-led perspective that can turn local market understanding into a differentiated edge

## The short thesis

The center of gravity in web3 business development has shifted.

The highest-value work is no longer "find hype, launch token, get listings."

The highest-value work is now:

- move money better
- tokenize assets with legal wrappers
- help institutions and enterprises enter the market safely
- sell compliance, custody, and operating reliability as part of the product
- build distribution around regulated use cases, not just protocol narratives

## What changed in 2025-2026

Several things moved from narrative to operating reality:

- Stablecoins became a mainstream product discussion across payments, treasury, settlement, and B2B cross-border workflows.
- Tokenization moved from theory toward actual regulated issuance infrastructure and distribution.
- Regulation became clearer in major markets, which means BD now needs to understand legal perimeter, bankability, and licensing constraints.
- Korea became more interesting not because of retail speculation alone, but because policy, banking, and enterprise experimentation started to matter more.

## How to use this guide

Read the chapters in order once.

Then use the book in three passes:

1. Market pass: understand where value is forming and which BMs deserve your time.
2. Operator pass: learn how to run pipeline, partner discovery, and account strategy in a way that closes real business.
3. Career pass: package your knowledge into a differentiated profile that compounds over time.

## What this book is trying to help you do

By the end, you should be able to:

- explain the hottest web3 BMs in plain business terms
- identify which opportunities are durable versus narrative-only
- speak credibly with fintechs, exchanges, banks, enterprises, and regulators
- build a 30-60-90 day BD plan
- create your own view on what Korea can export into the global market

## A note on "hot"

"Hot" does not mean noisy.

In this book, "hot" means one or more of:

- budgets are opening
- regulation is clarifying
- enterprise buyers are active
- take rates can be explained
- the product has repeatable operational value
- the category is likely to still matter in 24 months
