# Scorecards And Templates

## Opportunity scorecard

Score each opportunity from 1 to 5 on:

- pain intensity
- budget owner clarity
- regulatory feasibility
- bankability
- implementation burden
- expansion potential
- strategic reference value

Interpretation:

- 28 and above: strong priority
- 22 to 27: keep in motion but control scope
- 21 and below: likely distraction unless strategically necessary

## Partner scorecard

Ask:

- Do they bring distribution?
- Do they reduce legal or regulatory friction?
- Do they improve liquidity or bank access?
- Do they accelerate implementation?
- Do they increase trust with the end buyer?

If the answer is "no" to most of these, the partnership is weak.

## Discovery template

Use this structure in calls:

1. Current workflow
2. Current pain and where it shows up financially
3. Existing workaround
4. Internal stakeholders
5. Compliance and operational blockers
6. Timing and decision trigger
7. Next step with a clearly owned action

## Pilot design template

Define:

- customer segment
- use case
- geography or corridor
- fund flow
- compliance responsibility
- exact KPI targets
- success condition for expansion
- timeline and resourcing

## Objection handling framework

Common objections:

- legal is not comfortable
- treasury does not want crypto exposure
- integration looks heavy
- there is no urgent need right now
- a bank partner may reject it

Response pattern:

- clarify the specific blocker
- reduce scope
- shift from ideology to economics and controls
- introduce a regulated or trusted partner where relevant
- create an option structure, not one all-or-nothing proposal

## Account plan template

For each top target, write:

- why this account now
- what pain is most likely
- who likely owns the budget
- who blocks the deal
- what reference would matter to them
- what the smallest credible pilot is
- what the second sale would be after the first

## Meeting note template

- objective
- what was learned
- what remains unclear
- blockers
- political map
- next action
- owner
- deadline

## Weekly BD review

Review these every week:

- new pipeline created
- qualified pipeline value
- deals advanced
- deals downgraded or killed
- blocker themes
- product asks created by real demand
- legal or operational gaps slowing closure

## Personal scorecard

Each month, score yourself from 1 to 5 on:

- market knowledge
- regulatory fluency
- quality of discovery
- deal design quality
- executive communication
- internal cross-functional leadership
- writing and thought leadership

You improve faster when you can see where you are actually weak.
