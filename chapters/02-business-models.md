# Business Models That Matter

## How to evaluate a web3 BM

Use six filters:

- buyer urgency
- willingness to pay
- regulatory clarity
- distribution difficulty
- gross margin quality
- durability after hype fades

The best 2026 models usually score well on at least four of the six.

## 1. Stablecoin payments infrastructure

### Core customer

- fintechs
- payment service providers
- exchanges
- global payroll and payout platforms
- marketplaces
- exporters and importers

### Revenue model

- transaction fees
- FX spread
- treasury and float income
- SaaS platform fees
- premium compliance or routing fees

### Why it works

- The pain is concrete.
- Savings can be measured against SWIFT, correspondent banking, and trapped working capital.
- Customers already have payment budgets.

### What creates moat

- banking relationships
- corridor liquidity
- licensing position
- compliance stack
- enterprise integrations

### Key KPIs

- payment volume
- active corridors
- time to settlement
- take rate by corridor
- loss or incident rate
- conversion from quote to funded flow

### Korea angle

Strong for:

- exporter settlement
- creator and gaming payouts
- overseas contractor payroll
- treasury operations for companies with global vendors

## 2. RWA and tokenization rails

### Core customer

- asset managers
- issuers
- brokerages
- securities firms
- custodians
- banks

### Revenue model

- issuance fees
- admin and servicing fees
- custody fees
- transfer and compliance fees
- marketplace transaction fees

### Why it works

- It converts complex, manual operations into programmable workflows.
- It can lower minimum ticket size and widen distribution.
- It creates recurring operational revenue after issuance.

### What creates moat

- legal and compliance design
- issuer distribution
- regulated market access
- integration with custody and transfer restrictions

### Key KPIs

- assets issued
- active investors
- issuer retention
- average servicing revenue per asset
- time to onboard a new issuer

### Korea angle

Strong for:

- STO infrastructure
- receivables and invoice-backed products
- culture, media, and IP-linked structuring if legally sound
- distribution partnerships with regulated financial players

## 3. Custody and governance stack

### Core customer

- enterprises
- funds
- trading firms
- banks exploring digital asset operations
- foundations and protocols with treasury controls

### Revenue model

- SaaS seat or wallet fees
- assets under custody fees
- policy engine upsells
- implementation and support

### Why it works

- It addresses board-level risk concerns.
- Procurement can be justified under security and control budgets.
- It becomes sticky after policies and approvals are embedded.

### Key moat

- security reputation
- regulatory posture
- integrations with exchanges, banks, and workflows
- audit readiness

## 4. Compliance and transaction intelligence

### Core customer

- exchanges
- custodians
- fintechs
- banks
- OTC desks

### Revenue model

- subscription
- usage-based screening fees
- enterprise contracts

### Why it works

- Mandatory spend is better than optional spend.
- As regulation matures, compliance budgets grow faster than narrative budgets.

### What creates moat

- proprietary entity labels
- investigator workflow
- trust with regulators and compliance teams

## 5. Staking, validator, and yield infrastructure

### Core customer

- institutions
- funds
- wallets
- protocols

### Revenue model

- staking commission
- white-label infrastructure fees
- reporting and institutional support fees

### Why it works

- There is recurring revenue and operational stickiness.
- Institutions need reliable operators, not hobbyist infrastructure.

### The caution

- This market can commoditize.
- Margin defense requires reliability, distribution, or premium service.

## 6. Developer tooling and infrastructure

### When it works

- when embedded in customer workflow
- when linked to enterprise adoption
- when sold with reliability or compliance outcomes

### When it fails

- when the tool is technically elegant but not required for budget approval
- when the buyer is another startup with no durable spending power

## Ranking the hottest BMs for 2026

If the question is commercial heat plus durability, the order is:

1. Stablecoin payments and treasury infrastructure
2. Tokenization, RWA operations, and STO rails
3. Custody, compliance, and enterprise controls
4. Institutional staking and validator services with distribution
5. Specialized data and intelligence products
6. Infrastructure that has distribution attached

## Mental model for BD

A BM is good if you can answer three questions clearly:

- Who pays?
- Why now?
- Why you instead of another provider or an internal build?

If those answers are vague, the category is probably still too early or too crowded.
