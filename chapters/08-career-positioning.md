# Career Positioning

## The role you are building toward

If you want to become a strong senior BD operator, head of BD, or strategic partnerships lead in web3, your profile should combine four things:

- market judgment
- commercial execution
- regulatory awareness
- narrative clarity

Most candidates have one or two. Few have all four.

## How to stand out

## 1. Build a clear market thesis

Be known for a point of view, not for generic interest in blockchain.

A good thesis sounds like:

- "Stablecoin-enabled treasury and cross-border settlement will be the strongest enterprise adoption wedge in Asia."
- "Korea can export tokenization infrastructure if products are designed around regulated financial distribution."

## 2. Speak in business language

Executives do not need more crypto jargon.

They need:

- revenue logic
- cost logic
- risk logic
- implementation logic

If you can translate web3 into those terms, you become more valuable immediately.

## 3. Publish useful thinking

Write short memos or public posts about:

- market shifts
- regulatory implications
- company strategy teardowns
- Korea versus global comparisons

This compounds credibility and sharpens your own thinking.

## 4. Collect operating proof

Track your real work:

- accounts opened
- pilots designed
- partnerships signed
- corridor launches
- process improvements
- revenue influenced

Outcomes beat vague networking claims.

## Your personal moat

For a Korea-based operator, a strong personal moat could be:

- Korea fluency plus global market understanding
- enterprise sales discipline plus crypto-native context
- payments and tokenization knowledge plus partnership execution

That combination is rare enough to matter.

## Suggested career assets

Build these:

- one market thesis memo
- one Korea-outward opportunity paper
- one sample account plan
- one sample pilot design
- one list of market observations updated monthly

## How to talk in interviews or senior meetings

Use this structure:

1. Here is the market shift.
2. Here is where the buyer pain is real.
3. Here is the business model that captures value.
4. Here is the operating risk.
5. Here is how I would build pipeline and close motion.

That sounds like an operator.

## What to avoid

- vague claims about "community growth" without revenue or strategic impact
- overuse of token price narratives
- confusing partner logos with actual distribution
- talking about web3 adoption without specifying workflow and buyer

## Long-term goal

Over the next two to three years, aim to become the person who can do all of the following:

- read a market shift clearly
- identify which account types it creates
- frame the legal and operating constraints
- design a credible commercial motion
- communicate it in board-safe language

That is a durable senior BD profile.
